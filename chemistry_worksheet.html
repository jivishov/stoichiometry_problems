LiOH Production
Easy

Determine the mass of lithium hydroxide (\(\text{LiOH}\)) produced when \(0.38 \text{ g}\) of lithium nitride (\(\text{Li}_3\text{N}\)) reacts with water according to the following equation:

$$\text{Li}_3\text{N} + 3\text{H}_2\text{O} \longrightarrow \text{NH}_3 + 3\text{LiOH}$$
Show Solution
✅ Solution to Problem 1
$$\text{Li}_3\text{N} + 3\text{H}_2\text{O} \longrightarrow \text{NH}_3 + 3\text{LiOH}$$ $$ 0.38 \text{ g } \text{Li}_3\text{N} \times \frac{1 \text{ mol } \text{Li}_3\text{N}}{35.0 \text{ g}} \times \frac{3 \text{ mol } \text{LiOH}}{1 \text{ mol } \text{Li}_3\text{N}} \times \frac{23.95 \text{ g } \text{LiOH}}{1 \text{ mol } \text{LiOH}}$$ $$\approx \mathbf{0.78 \text{ g } \text{LiOH}}$$

Limiting Reagent (Precipitation)
Medium

Identify the limiting reagent when \(65.14 \text{ g}\) of calcium chloride (\(\text{CaCl}_2\)) reacts with \(74.68 \text{ g}\) of sodium carbonate (\(\text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3\)) to produce calcium carbonate (\(\text{CaCO}_3\)) and sodium chloride (\(\text{NaCl}\)).

Show Solution
✅ Solution to Problem 7
$$\text{CaCl}_2 + \text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3 \longrightarrow \text{CaCO}_3 + 2\text{NaCl}$$ Mass NaCl from CaCl₂: $$ 65.14 \text{ g } \text{CaCl}_2 \times \frac{1 \text{ mol } \text{CaCl}_2}{110.98 \text{ g}} \times \frac{2 \text{ mol } \text{NaCl}}{1 \text{ mol } \text{CaCl}_2} \times \frac{58.44 \text{ g } \text{NaCl}}{1 \text{ mol } \text{NaCl}}$$ $$\approx 68.62 \text{ g } \text{NaCl}$$ Mass NaCl from Na₂CO₃: $$ 74.68 \text{ g } \text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3 \times \frac{1 \text{ mol } \text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3}{105.99 \text{ g}} \times \frac{2 \text{ mol } \text{NaCl}}{1 \text{ mol } \text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3} \times \frac{58.44 \text{ g } \text{NaCl}}{1 \text{ mol } \text{NaCl}}$$ $$\approx 82.47 \text{ g } \text{NaCl}$$ Conclusion: CaCl₂ is the limiting reagent (produces less product).

Limiting Reagent (IF₅ and SO₂)
Medium

Identify the limiting reactant when \(4.687 \text{ g}\) of sulfur tetrafluoride (\(\text{SF}_4\)) reacts with \(6.281 \text{ g}\) of diiodine pentoxide (\(\text{I}_2\text{O}_5\)) to produce iodine pentafluoride (\(\text{IF}_5\)) and sulfur dioxide (\(\text{SO}_2\)).

Show Solution
✅ Solution to Problem 8
$$5\text{SF}_4 + 2\text{I}_2\text{O}_5 \longrightarrow 4\text{IF}_5 + 5\text{SO}_2$$ Mass SO₂ from SF₄ (MM ≈ 108.06 g/mol): $$ 4.687 \text{ g } \text{SF}_4 \times \frac{1 \text{ mol } \text{SF}_4}{108.06 \text{ g}} \times \frac{5 \text{ mol } \text{SO}_2}{5 \text{ mol } \text{SF}_4} \times \frac{64.07 \text{ g } \text{SO}_2}{1 \text{ mol } \text{SO}_2}$$ $$\approx 2.775 \text{ g } \text{SO}_2$$ Mass SO₂ from I₂O₅ (MM ≈ 333.81 g/mol): $$ 6.281 \text{ g } \text{I}_2\text{O}_5 \times \frac{1 \text{ mol } \text{I}_2\text{O}_5}{333.81 \text{ g}} \times \frac{5 \text{ mol } \text{SO}_2}{2 \text{ mol } \text{I}_2\text{O}_5} \times \frac{64.07 \text{ g } \text{SO}_2}{1 \text{ mol } \text{SO}_2}$$ $$\approx 3.011 \text{ g } \text{SO}_2$$ Conclusion: SF₄ is the limiting reactant.

If \(4.1 \text{ g}\) of chromium (\(\text{Cr}\)) is heated with \(9.3 \text{ g}\) of chlorine gas (\(\text{Cl}_2\)), what mass of chromium(III) chloride (\(\text{CrCl}_3\)) will be produced?

Show Solution
✅ Solution to Problem 9
$$2\text{Cr} + 3\text{Cl}_2 \longrightarrow 2\text{CrCl}_3$$ Mass CrCl₃ from Cr (MM ≈ 52.00 g/mol): $$ 4.1 \text{ g } \text{Cr} \times \frac{1 \text{ mol } \text{Cr}}{52.00 \text{ g}} \times \frac{2 \text{ mol } \text{CrCl}_3}{2 \text{ mol } \text{Cr}} \times \frac{158.36 \text{ g } \text{CrCl}_3}{1 \text{ mol } \text{CrCl}_3}$$ $$\approx 12.5 \text{ g } \text{CrCl}_3$$ Mass CrCl₃ from Cl₂: $$ 9.3 \text{ g } \text{Cl}_2 \times \frac{1 \text{ mol } \text{Cl}_2}{70.91 \text{ g}} \times \frac{2 \text{ mol } \text{CrCl}_3}{3 \text{ mol } \text{Cl}_2} \times \frac{158.36 \text{ g } \text{CrCl}_3}{1 \text{ mol } \text{CrCl}_3}$$ $$\approx 13.8 \text{ g } \text{CrCl}_3$$ Conclusion: Cr is the limiting reagent, producing 13 g CrCl₃ (2 sig figs).

Nitrogen Compound Identification
Hard

Hydroxylammonium chloride (\(\text{NH}_3\text{OH}^+\text{Cl}^-\)) reacts with iron(III) chloride (\(\text{FeCl}_3\)) in solution to produce iron(II) chloride (\(\text{FeCl}_2\)), \(\text{HCl}\), \(\text{H}_2\text{O}\), and a compound of nitrogen. It was found that \(2.00 \text{ g}\) of iron(III) chloride reacted in this way with \(31.0 \text{ mL}\) of \(0.200 \text{ M}\) hydroxylammonium chloride. Suggest a possible formula for the compound of nitrogen so produced.

Show Solution
✅ Solution to Problem 14
Moles NH₃OH⁺Cl⁻ = 0.0310 L × 0.200 M = 0.0062 mol Moles FeCl₃ = 2.00 g / 162.204 g/mol ≈ 0.01233 mol The molar ratio FeCl₃ : NH₃OH⁺Cl⁻ is approximately 2:1. Fe³⁺ gains 1 electron (Fe³⁺ → Fe²⁺). Since 2 Fe³⁺ are reduced per 1 N atom, the N atom must lose 2 electrons. Initial oxidation state of N in NH₃OH⁺Cl⁻ is -1. Final oxidation state = (-1) - (loss of 2e⁻) = +1. A compound with N in the +1 oxidation state is N₂O. Possible Formula: N₂O

Gravimetric Analysis of a Mixture (1)
Hard

A mixture consisting of only sodium chloride (\(\text{NaCl}\)) and potassium chloride (\(\text{KCl}\)) weighs \(1.0000 \text{ g}\). When the mixture is dissolved in water and an excess of silver nitrate is added, all the chloride ions are precipitated as insoluble silver chloride (\(\text{AgCl}\)). The mass of the silver chloride is found to be \(2.1476 \text{ g}\). Calculate the mass percentages of sodium chloride and potassium chloride in the original mixture.

Show Solution
✅ Solution to Problem 16
Let x = mass of NaCl. (1.0000 - x) = mass of KCl. MMs: NaCl (58.44), KCl (74.55), AgCl (143.32). Set up mass balance based on AgCl precipitation: $$ \frac{x}{58.44} \times 143.32 + \frac{(1.0000 - x)}{74.55} \times 143.32 = 2.1476 $$ $$ 2.452x + 1.922(1 - x) = 2.1476 $$ $$ 0.530x = 0.2256 $$ $$ x \approx 0.4257 \text{ g NaCl} $$ Mass NaCl = 0.4257 g → 42.57% Mass KCl = 1.0000 - 0.4257 = 0.5743 g → 57.43%

Hard

Assuming \(100\%\) yield, what mass of ammonia will be produced from a \(1:1\) molar ratio mixture in a reactor that has a volume of \(8.75 \times 10^3 \text{ L}\) under a total pressure of \(2.75 \times 10^7 \text{ Pa}\) at \(455^\circ\text{C}\)?

Show Solution
✅ Solution to Problem 17

Use PV = nRT to find moles of H₂: (135.702)(8.75 × 10³) = n(0.08206)(728.15) n_H₂ ≈ 19876.1 mol Moles NH₃ (3:2 ratio): 19876.1 × (2/3) ≈ 13250.7 mol Mass NH₃ = 13250.7 mol × 17.031 g/mol ≈ 225669 g Mass NH₃ produced: 2.26 × 10⁵ g

Hard

Water is added to \(4.267 \text{ g}\) of \(\text{UF}_6\). The only products are \(3.730 \text{ g}\) of a solid containing only uranium, oxygen, and fluorine (\(\text{U}\), \(\text{O}\), \(\text{F}\)) and \(0.970 \text{ g}\) of a gas. The gas is \(95.0\%\) fluorine and the remainder is hydrogen.

(a) What fraction of the fluorine of the original is in the solid and what fraction in the gas after the reaction?

Show Solution
✅ Solution to Problem 22

Mass U_solid = 3.730 - 0.9215 (in gas) - 0.3845 (O) ≈ 2.8853 g. 4. Moles (U: 0.01212) : (F: 0.02422) : (O: 0.02403) 5. Ratio ≈ 1:2:2. Formula of the Solid Product: UF₂O₂
